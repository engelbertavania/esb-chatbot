Background
ESB Order merchants currently rely on a fully manual 8-step support process to resolve operational issues. The majority of these issues — including QR code failures, menu visibility problems, payment configuration errors, and printer disconnections — are repetitive, structured, and follow predictable resolution paths. Despite this, every interaction is handled manually, creating avoidable workload for the Customer Support (CS) team and unnecessary downtime for merchants.
Opportunity
By introducing an AI-powered chatbot in Phase 1, ESB Order can automate the first layer of support for high-frequency operational issues. The chatbot will guide merchants through structured troubleshooting flows and automatically generate support tickets with full conversation context when automation cannot resolve the issue.
This approach reduces repetitive manual work for the CS team, shortens merchant resolution time, and provides a consistent 24/7 first-response experience without depending on agent availability.
Key opportunities include:
Reduce repetitive support interactions handled manually by CS
Improve merchant self-service resolution capability
Shorten issue handling and resolution time
Improve ticket quality through structured issue collection
Build structured conversation datasets for future AI model improvements
Establish the technical foundation for WhatsApp and advanced AI support in Phase 2

Known Limitations & Constraints
Image-based issue classification is not supported in Phase 1
AI responses are limited to predefined ESB Product issue categories and troubleshooting flows
Freeform AI troubleshooting outside the configured knowledge base is out of scope
Chatbot channel support is limited to internal/API integration during Phase 1
WhatsApp Business integration will be delivered in Phase 2
Analytics dashboard and advanced reporting are not included in Phase 1 will be delivered in Phase 2
Session history is retained only within a 30-minute active session window
Cross-session memory and persistent conversation history are not supported
Layer 2 technical diagnostics and proactive monitoring are out of scope
Attachment upload supports image & video only in Phase 1
AI classification accuracy depends on the quality and completeness of the training data provided by ESB teams
Ticket routing depends on existing ticketing system integration availability
Multi-language NLP optimization is not included in Phase 1
Voice input and voice response interactions are not supported
Internet connectivity is required for chatbot interaction and ticket submission
